Chicken Lo Mein

This meal is a savory and satisfying stir-fry featuring tender marinated chicken or beef, mixed with fresh vegetables and perfectly cooked lo mein noodles, all coated in a rich and flavorful sauce with hints of ginger and garlic. It's a delicious and balanced Asian-inspired dish that's sure to satisfy your taste buds.

Ingredients

    Chicken

  • 8 oz chicken
  • ¼ t baking soda
  • ¼ t cornstarch (0.75 gram)
  • ¼ t sugar (1.25 gram)
  • Pinch of salt
  • ¼ t light soy sauce (1.5 grams)
  • ½ t Shaoxing wine (2.5 grams)
  • ½ t neutral oil (2.25 grams)

    • Sauce Ingredients

  • 1 T cornstarch (9.5 grams)
  • 1 ½ tsp sugar (7.5 grams)
  • ¼ t white pepper (0.6 grams)
  • ½ tsp kosher salt (1.5 grams)
  • ¼ cup chicken stock (60ml)
  • 2 T light soy sauce (30ml)
  • 1 t dark soy sauce (5ml)
  • 2 T oyster sauce (30ml)
  • 1/2 tsp toasted sesame oil (5ml)

  • 16 oz lo mein noodles
  • 1 cup (70 gram) Cabbage
  • ¼ cup (30 gram) Onion, thinly sliced
  • ¼ cup (20 gram) Carrot
  • 1 T (6 gram) ginger, minced
  • 1 T (8.5 gram) garlic, minced
  • 1 T Shaoxing wine (15ml)
  • 2-4 Scallion, 1-inch slices
  • ½ cup (45 grams) Bean Sprouts

Instructions

    For the beef

  1. Rinse the beef under cold running water for 1 minute, then drain well.
  2. Mix in baking soda and massage it into the meat for 1 minute.
  3. Mix in the other marinade ingredients and marinate for 15 minutes or up to overnight.

    4. For the sauce

  4. Mix all the dry sauce ingredients together.
  5. Stir in the chicken stock and whisk until all dry ingredients are dissolved.
  6. Mix in the wet sauce ingredients and set aside. (Stir before using).

    7. For the noodles

  7. Follow the package instructions to prepare the noodles.
  8. Drain them well and toss with oil.

    9. Cooking the dish

  9. Heat a wok or pan over medium-high heat.
  10. Add oil and swirl it around until it shimmers.
  11. Add cabbage, onion, and carrot, and sauté for 1 minute. Set aside.
  12. Return the wok or pan to medium-high heat and add 2 tablespoons of oil.
  13. Once the oil is hot, add the marinated beef to the pan and press it down into a single layer. Cook until the beef is nicely browned, then set it aside.
  14. Return the wok or pan to medium-high heat.
  15. Add 1 tablespoon of oil, 1 tablespoon of ginger, and 1 tablespoon of garlic. Sauté for 10-20 seconds.
  16. Add the prepared noodles and toss them with the ginger and garlic, mixing until the noodles are pliable.
  17. Add the sautéed vegetables and cooked beef to the noodles, tossing until thoroughly mixed.
  18. Move the noodles to one side of the pan and deglaze the other side with 1 tablespoon of Shaoxing wine. Toss the wine with the noodles.
  19. Stir the sauce mixture again to make sure the cornstarch hasn't settled at the bottom.
  20. Move the noodles to one side of the pan again and add the sauce to the empty side of the pan, not on top of the noodles. Allow it to cook for 15-20 seconds to thicken, then turn off the heat.
  21. Stir the noodles into the sauce.
  22. Add green onions and bean sprouts, mixing thoroughly.
  23. Serve and enjoy!
