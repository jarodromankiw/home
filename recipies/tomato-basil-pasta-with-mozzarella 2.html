Blueberry Streusel Cake

This blueberry streusel cake is a delicious and moist treat, perfect for breakfast or dessert. The crumbly streusel topping adds a delightful crunch to every bite.

Ingredients

    For the Streusel Topping

  • 80 g packed light brown sugar
  • 65 g all-purpose flour, spooned into measuring cup and leveled-off
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 55 g unsalted butter, cold, cut into ½-inch chunks

    • For the Cake

  • 260 g all-purpose flour, spooned into measuring cup and leveled-off
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 110 g unsalted butter, softened
  • 150 g granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1½ teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon packed lemon zest, from 1 lemon
  • 120 ml milk
  • 280 g fresh blueberries (frozen may be used but do not defrost)

Instructions

    For the Streusel Topping

  1. In a medium bowl, combine the brown sugar, flour, cinnamon, and salt.
  2. Add the cold butter chunks and use your fingers or a pastry cutter to work the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Set aside.

    3. For the Cake

  3. Preheat your oven to 175°C (350°F). Grease and flour a 9-inch square baking pan.
  4. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Set aside.
  5. In a large bowl, cream the softened butter and granulated sugar together until light and fluffy.
  6. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Mix in the vanilla extract and lemon zest.
  7. Alternately add the flour mixture and milk to the butter mixture, starting and ending with the flour mixture. Mix just until combined.
  8. Gently fold in the blueberries, being careful not to overmix.
  9. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and spread evenly. Sprinkle the streusel topping evenly over the batter.
  10. Bake for 40-45 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the cake comes out clean.
  11. Allow the cake to cool in the pan before slicing and serving.
