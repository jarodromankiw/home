Tomato Soup

Vine-ripened tomatoes pureed with fresh cream for a velvety smooth flavor accented by hints of red pepper and oregano and topped with black pepper focaccia croutons.

Ingredients

    Soup

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 large yellow onion, sliced
  • 1 (28 oz) can San Marzano peeled tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup chicken stock
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 1/8 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 4 large fresh basil leaves, roughly chopped
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/4 teaspoon of red pepper flakes
  • kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup shredded parmesan cheese (optional)

Instructions

    For the soup

  1. In a heavy bottomed pot (or dutch oven), heat olive oil over medium-high heat. Add garlic and saute for 30 seconds - until fragrant. Add onion and cook, stirring as needed, until translucent - about 8 minutes.
  2. Add San Marzano peeled tomatoes (juice and all), chicken stock, and sugar. Bring to a low simmer. Cook uncovered, for 12 minutes or until it has thickened.
  3. Add the milk, basil, oregano, red pepper flakes, butter and season to taste with salt and pepper.
  4. OPTIONAL: stir in parmesan cheese and serve topped with buttery croutons.
  5. Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days or in the freezer for up to 3 months.
